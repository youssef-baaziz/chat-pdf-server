World War I (1914 -1918), also known as the Great War, was a global conflict involving major 
world powers. Triggered by the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand, the war saw the 
Allies (led by France, Russia, and the UK) facing the Central Powers (led by Germany, 
Austri aHungary, and the Ottoman Empire). Trench warfare dominated the Western Front, with 
soldiers enduring harsh conditions. The conflict introduced new technologies such as 
machine guns and tanks. The war's devastating impact and unresolved issues set the stag e 
for World War II and reshaped the geopolitical landscape. It officially ended on November 11, 
1918, with the signing of the Armistice.
